Senior Year

Congrats!

You made it to senior year! Give yourself a pat on the back for all the hard work you've done over the last three years and get ready to make your last year your best one yet!

Making the Most of Senior Year:

  • Make note of all the things you meant to do during your time in college but have yet to get the chance. This can be anything: restaurants , hikes, picnics on campus lawns, rooftops of buildings, weekend adventures, and more!
  • If possible, try and fit any classes you really wanted to take but haven't yet.
  • Some of popular Cal professors and classes: Wealth & Poverty - Robert Reich, Intro to the Universe - Alex Filippenko, Intro to Climate Science - David Romps (Sana's personal favorite), classes from Hatem Bazian, CompSci 161 - Cybersecurity, CompSci 188 - Intro to AI, CompSci 189 - Intro to Machine Learning
  • Other popular & famous professors: Nick Weaver (CS), Dan Garcia (CS), Anant Sahai (EECS), Jonathan Shewchuk (CS), Josh Hug (CS), John DeNero (CS & DS), Ani Adhikari (DS), Ken Ribet (Math), Lea Beneish (Math), Gabriel Dorfsman-Hopkins (Math), Edward Frenkel (Math), Nick Miller (Math).

Your Career & Future:

  • As a senior, chances are you're recruiting for full time roles if you don't already have one slated for after graduation. And if you aren't recruiting for full-time, you may be prepping for graduate studies in a masters, PhD, law, or medical school. These are just the two most common pathways. If you aren't doing either, your journey is just as valid and everyone's path is different!
  • For those of you recruiting full-time, you've likely built your résumé up over the last 3 years in college and will be a solid applicant to the roles within your industry. If you're applying for post-grad programs, you are most likely finishing up standardized testing or your application itself, getting letters of recommendation, and planning out your application process in general.
  • To learn more about the post-grad plans of seniors at UC Berkeley across various majors check out this survey from the Career Center: First Destination Survey
